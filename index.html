We're here to answer your questions

While solar panels are most efficient in direct sunlight, they can still generate electricity on cloudy or rainy days. Solar panels can still capture diffuse sunlight and convert it into electricity, although their output may be reduced compared to sunny days. Additionally, your solar panel system may be connected to the grid, allowing you to draw electricity from the grid when solar production is low.

Solar panels generally require minimal maintenance. Periodic cleaning to remove dirt, dust, and debris can help maximize their efficiency. Additionally, it's essential to monitor your system's performance regularly and schedule professional inspections every few years to ensure everything is functioning correctly. Most reputable solar panel manufacturers offer warranties covering equipment and performance for 25 years or more, providing peace of mind for homeowners.

The amount of money you'll save with solar panels depends on several factors, including your location, energy usage, and the size of your solar panel system. Generally, homeowners can expect to save thousands of dollars over the lifespan of their solar panels, with savings typically ranging from 10% to 30% off their electricity bills.
